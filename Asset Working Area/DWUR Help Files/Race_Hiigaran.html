Weapon Preference: Railguns, Area-of-Effect and Fighters

The Hiigarans are a species shrouded in controversy.

During the darkest times in the ancient galaxy, when war losses to the Shakturi were enormous, the Humans were desperate to replenish their ranks. They covertly attempted to splice Securan DNA into unsuspecting participants to take advantage of the Securans genetic propensity for reproduction.

The results were somewhat successful, producing an intelligent and fertile species. But the new hybrids were genetically unstable, prone to certian diseases and developmental challenges.

When this became known to the Securan State, the Human/Securan alliance was almost shattered beyond repair.

The Securans demanded the destruction of the hybrids, but the Humans resisted. A deal was struck where the few thousand hybrids were sent to the far reaches of the Galaxy. They were transplanted to the desert planet Kharak and left to fend for themselves. Ironically, it was this exile that probably spared the hybrids from eradication at the hands of the eventual victorious Shakturi.

The hybrids (later calling themselves Hiigarans) adapted to the harsh climate through sheer inginuity and reproductive capacity. As their civilization re-entered space, contact with scattered remnants of other races began. An unsuspected discovery of the ability to mate with Humans, Securans, and Xhumans has led to a population explosion.

The Hiigarans are friendly even though they have every right not to be. They are fatalists, seeing their history as the intended will of some larger force. They hold no ill will towards Humans or Securans, though those factions are less amenable.

Despite their genetic compatibility with several other races, Hiigarans appearance stays surprisingly close to those who spent those centuries in exile on Kharak.
